 Program Fulbright-Hays

Students embark on immersive excursions to iconic Bolivian destinations such as the Salar de Uyuni, Madidi National Park, Potosí, and Lake Titicaca. These experiences seamlessly blend tourism with academic exploration, allowing students to enjoy Bolivia’s breathtaking landscapes while deepening their understanding of its cultural, historical, and environmental significance.

In workshops led by expert faculty, students explore critical topics such as public health, politics, and community-based research. These interactive sessions provide students with hands-on experience, enabling them to apply theoretical knowledge to real-world challenges.

Through this practical approach, students gain valuable insights into the complexities of Bolivian society, fostering a deeper and more nuanced understanding of its realities and cultural context.

Students will participate in a cultural immersion program, living with carefully selected Bolivian host families. This daily interaction provides firsthand experience of Bolivian customs and traditions.

The Waliki Friends student volunteer network creates spaces for integration between international students and the local community through in-person recreational and cultural activities.
